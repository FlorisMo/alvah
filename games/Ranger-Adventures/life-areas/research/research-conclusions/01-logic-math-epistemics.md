## 1. Verdict — what we build on

1. **Three moves are well-evidenced and worth building first** (*zeker*): build **number sense
   before procedure** (concrete→pictorial→abstract), train **calibration** via repeated
   confidence-rating-with-feedback, and teach **verification habits** (lateral reading). Everything
   else is secondary.
2. **Calibration is our highest-leverage, best-evidenced epistemics mechanic** — strong in adults,
   *waarschijnlijk* in children, and it scores cleanly (Brier). This is the flagship build (§6).
3. **Far-transfer is the big honest caveat** (*zeker* that it's mostly absent). "Train logic/
   critical-thinking and the child reasons better in general" is **not established**. Design for
   *specific* skills repeated across many concrete domains; never promise general "smarter thinking."
   Anchor: Kassai et al. (2019), *Psychological Bulletin* — near-transfer g⁺ = 0.44 (real),
   far-transfer g⁺ = 0.11 (non-significant). Same lesson the EF research already taught us.
4. **Teaching a child to catch confident AI hallucination has essentially NO efficacy evidence.**
   Build it (it matters most for the AI era) but **label it experimental**; lean on the validated
   *lateral-reading* lineage as the transferable backbone, not AI-specific frameworks.
5. **Developmental ages are softer than Piaget claimed.** Conditional reasoning appears by ~3 with
   concrete stimuli; competence is task- and presentation-dependent, not stage-locked. Don't gate by
   age alone — gate by concreteness and scaffolding.

## 2. What this trains, and why (design intent)

Valid reasoning that survives a fluent-but-wrong machine; number sense as the spine under math; and
**epistemics — how you know, and how sure you should be** — as the defining AI-era skill against
confident nonsense. Diegetic fit is **mix** for both: track-reading and "what explains these signs?"
inference live in-world; pure math drills and the calibration meter are a light meta practice screen.

## 3. The spiral 7→21

**Reasoning/logic**

| Age | Concept | What it is | Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7–8 | Sort / sequence / classify; simple if-then | Group by attribute; "if X then Y" rules | Realistic (conditionals emerge ~3 with concrete stimuli) |
| 7–9 | Transitive inference | "A>B, B>C ⇒ A>C" with visual support | Realistic w/ visuals; abstract verbal harder |
| 8–10 | Cause vs correlation (intuitive) | "Did X cause Y or just co-occur?" | Realistic as intuition; formal CVS later |
| 9–11 | Simple proof / "why must this be true" | Necessity vs observation; counterexamples | Borderline — concrete yes, abstract necessity pushed-too-early before ~11 |
| 10–12 | Counterfactual (constrained) | "If B hadn't happened, all else fixed…" | Realistic 9–12 for overdetermined cases |
| 11–13 | Control-of-variables / hypothesis testing | Vary one thing at a time | Realistic from ~11–12 |
| 12–15 | Conditional logic formalised; **validity vs truth** | An argument can be valid but false | Realistic adolescent |
| 14–18 | Probabilistic / Bayesian-flavoured | Update belief by degree on evidence | Realistic if built on earlier frequency intuitions |
| 16–21 | Formal/abstract argument; meta-logic | Reasoning about reasoning; quantifiers | Realistic late |

**Math (number sense before procedure)**

| Age | Concept | What it is | Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7–8 | Number bonds / part-whole; subitising | Seeing 7 as 5+2 | Foundational |
| 7–9 | CPA for +/− | Concrete → pictorial (bar/number line) → symbol | Realistic; don't rush pictorial |
| 8–10 | Multiplicative thinking; arrays | Groups-of; area model before table-grind | Realistic |
| 8–11 | Fact fluency / automaticity | Fast retrieval via **spaced, low-stakes** recall | Realistic IF humane; high-stakes timed drills risk anxiety |
| 9–12 | Bar-model problem solving (Singapore) | Visual algebra-precursor | Realistic, strong |
| 10–13 | Fractions/ratio via models | Equivalence via paper-strip/bar | Realistic; symbol-first too early |
| 12–15 | Pre-algebra / generalising patterns | Letters as generalised numbers | Realistic |
| 14–21 | Formal algebra → proof/calculus-readiness | Symbol manipulation with meaning | Realistic late |

**Epistemics**

| Age | Concept | What it is | Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7–8 | "How sure am I?" (3-point) | Sure / not sure / guessing | Realistic; keep coarse |
| 8–10 | Confidence 1–5 + outcome feedback | Rate, then see if right; notice over/under-confidence | Realistic; expect overconfidence baseline |
| 8–10 | "Where did I learn this?" source tagging | Saw it / told / worked it out | Realistic |
| 9–11 | Updating on evidence (frequency-framed) | Change mind by degree as counts shift | Realistic w/ **natural frequencies**; % framing too early |
| 10–12 | Base rates ("how common normally") | Don't ignore the background rate | Realistic w/ concrete framing |
| 11–13 | Steelman before disagreeing | State the other view at its strongest first | Realistic via examples |
| 11–14 | Judge a source (lateral reading) | Leave the page; check who's behind it | Realistic; validated from elementary up |
| 13–16 | Calibration as a measured habit | Track hit-rate vs stated confidence | Realistic |
| 14–18 | **Evaluating AI output** | Treat AI as a draft; "how would I check this?" | Practise yes; **evidence thin** |
| 16–21 | Probabilistic forecasting + scoring | Dated predictions, Brier, review | Realistic late |

## 5. Game mechanics to build

**Model A — scored/psychometric** (plug into the existing staircase + `scoring.js`):
- **Sure-O-Meter** — answer, set confidence, watch your personal calibration curve build. Brier-scored. *The flagship — see §6.*
- **Prediction Diary** — dated real-world predictions, resolved later, Brier over time.
- **One-Thing-At-A-Time (CVS)** — only fair tests (vary one variable) count; scored on proportion controlled.
- **Cause Detective** — design a tiny test to isolate which of two co-occurring things is causal; scored on isolation.
- **Source Sleuth / Lateral Reading** — "open tabs" to check who's behind a claim before judging; scored on credibility judgement.
- **Spot the Confident Robot** *(EXPERIMENTAL — §7)* — an in-game "AI" sometimes hallucinates confidently; flag suspicious claims and pick a check. Scored, but internally flagged unvalidated.
- **Humane Fact Dojo** — short daily **spaced-retrieval** bursts; race *your own past self*, never a clock-shaming leaderboard; missed facts resurface sooner. Tracked as a fluency map, **explicitly not** a high-stakes timed score.

**Model B — mastery** (rungs unlock, no averaging):
- **Sort-and-Rule** (deduce a hidden classification rule) · **If-Then Machines** (build gate chains; culminating in validity-vs-truth puzzles) · **Number-Bond Builder** (CPA) · **Bar-Model Story Solver** (Singapore) · **Evidence Tipper** (intuitive Bayes with a counts-jar) · **Steelman First** (must state the strongest opposing view before picking a side; rubric) · **Argument Map Studio** · **Fallacy Zoo** (creatures embodying flawed moves, met through examples first — far-transfer treated as unproven).

**Diegetic hooks (ranger):** Cause Detective / CVS / Evidence Tipper / Source Sleuth all reskin as
*"is this really a wolf's track? what explains these signs?"* field investigations. Math drills and
the calibration meter stay meta.

## 6. Flagship deep-dive — the calibration mechanic ("Sure-O-Meter")

Highest-value, best-evidenced, and CBT-free.

**Elicitation by age:** 7–9 = three faces (sure / unsure / guessing); 9–12 = 1–5 stars; 12+ = a
probability slider (50–100% for two-option, full 0–100% later).

**Scoring — Brier.** For a stated probability *p* on an event with outcome *o* (1/0):
**Brier = (p − o)².** 0 = perfect, **0.25 = always saying "50%"** (the friendly baseline to beat),
1.0 = confidently wrong. Average over many items. Brier is a *proper* scoring rule: minimised by
reporting your true belief, so it removes any incentive to over- or under-claim.

**Presentation rules (so it never discourages):**
- Never a raw "you lost" per item. Show a **calibration curve**: "When you said 4/5 sure, you were
  right 7/10 — so 4/5 was a bit too confident." It's a *map of self-knowledge*, not a grade.
- **Reward honesty explicitly:** a separate "calibration/honesty" meter distinct from a "knowledge"
  meter, so being well-calibrated is a win *even when wrong*.
- Children start **overconfident** — make the gap visible and shrinkable, not punished.
- Youngest: skip numbers — a "confidence jar" that fills with sureness, then a "was the jar right?" beat.

## 7. Honesty lines — what we must NOT claim or do

- **No general-intelligence / "smarter thinking" claims.** Far-transfer is unproven (§1.3). Market and
  design for specific skills across concrete domains only.
- **AI-hallucination detection is an open research bet** (*evidence essentially absent*). Even trained
  adults detect hallucinations at ~55% (≈44% on complex cases). Keep "Spot the Confident Robot"
  flagged experimental; the transferable, evidenced backbone is lateral reading on *web* sources.
- **Lateral-reading evidence is real but mostly pre/post, not all RCT-grade** — improving *web-source*
  judgement is *zeker*; transfer to AI output is *waarschijnlijk, unproven*.
- **Boaler's specific claims are contested** (alleged mis-citation; "timed tests → anxiety/brain"
  overstated). Adopt humane fluency (spaced, low-stakes, self-referenced) — neither high-stakes timed
  drills nor a no-practice stance. Automaticity via spaced retrieval is well-supported.
- **Fallacy-name knowledge ≠ thinking well** — teach implicitly through examples.

## 8. Accessibility (dyslexic 8-year-old)

Audio-first, text-light — the core loops (sorting, chance, confidence) are largely non-verbal, so
exploit that and **never gate a reasoning task behind reading load**. Symbols/manipulatives/jars/
faces over text. **Decouple reading skill from reasoning score.** Plain formatting (larger size,
generous letter/line spacing, short left-aligned lines, high contrast, sans-serif) + text-to-speech
on everything; treat special "dyslexia fonts" (OpenDyslexic) as *waarschijnlijk overstated* — rely on
layout + audio, not a font. Allow **spoken** answers/steelmans where feasible. (Consistent with the
existing EF a11y line: ≥56px targets, dual-channel feedback, reduced-motion.)
