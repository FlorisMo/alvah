## 1. Verdict — what we build on

1. **Quantitative readiness gates the whole curriculum** (*zeker*). Percentages/proportional
   reasoning consolidate ~11–14. **The #1 too-early trap: teaching compounding before percentage
   fluency** — it gets mis-encoded as *linear* ("illusion of linearity"). Build EF, habits, and
   concrete saving first; layer the math after. This becomes a hard **unlock gate** in the build (§5).
2. **Design as if behavior-change is hard** (*zeker*). Financial *knowledge* moves reliably;
   real-world *behavior* moves weakly, decays, sometimes nulls (Fernandes 2014 vs. Kaiser/Lusardi
   2022). Treat the game as **structured practice + a parent conversation**, never a guaranteed
   outcome. The active ingredient across the divided literature is the recurring money *conversation*.
3. **Delayed gratification is a trainable, situational skill — NOT a character verdict** (*zeker* on
   the reappraisal). Children rationally wait *less* when the world has proven unreliable. **Design
   implication that is non-negotiable: the game world must honor every promised reward, on schedule** —
   so patience is the rational choice, never a willpower morality test.
4. **Reject every gambling-shaped reward loop** (*zeker* on the psychology). Variable-ratio rewards,
   near-misses, loot-box/jackpot reveals, loss-disguised-as-win, double-or-nothing — these literally
   train compulsion and teach the opposite of stewardship. EV/risk are taught only with **explicit
   odds, transparent outcomes, reason-before-act, capped attempts** (§6, the bright line).
5. **Real stakes / learning-by-doing is the most promising transfer lever** (*waarschijnlijk*; causal
   evidence thin in affluent settings). Graduate gradually to a real account with a parent in the
   loop (§7).

## 4. Game mechanics to build (15 seeds)

**⚠ Build dependency:** **Percent Forge (#7) is a gate** — must be cleared before Compounding Garden,
Transparent-Odds Choice, and Fee Leak unlock. Encode this in the unlock graph.

**Model A — scored puzzles:**
- **Percent Forge** *(GATE)* — "X% of Y" in money contexts; harder ratios → discounts → interest rates.
- **Doubling Machine** — predict value after N doublings, then reveal the curve (grains/pennies/folds).
- **Opportunity-Cost Crossroads** — pick one option; game names the next-best forgone; closer values scale it.
- **Transparent-Odds Choice** — "70% of +10 else 0" vs "sure +6"; reason → choose → EV revealed. Odds always visible, capped attempts, **no near-miss/escalation**.
- **The Fee Leak** — same portfolio at 0.1% vs 1.5% over decades; the gap visualized (fees = negative compounding).
- **Wants-vs-Needs Sorter** — drag items into two bins under a budget cap; descriptive feedback, no randomness.

**Model B — mastery / portfolio:**
- **The Goal Jar** ("ran a saving goal to completion"; horizon lengthens with mastery; zero randomness).
- **Run-a-Budget** ("ran a budget"; cover fixed costs, allocate, survive a month; later add income volatility).
- **The Reliable World (Patience Quests)** — now-vs-later where later *always* pays; teaches waiting *strategies*. **The world never reneges.**
- **Compounding Garden** — savings grow as a tree each season on the new total; fees shown as pruning shears.
- **Ruin Island** — repeated favorable bets, but a wipe-out ends the run; learn fractional sizing. **The lesson is that reckless EV-chasing loses — explicitly anti-gambling.**
- **Inflation Tides** · **Index vs Picker** (rewards holding, not trading; modeled/historical-style returns, not slot payouts) · **Diversify the Harvest** (framed as lowering downside, not chasing jackpots) · **Graduation Ledger** (mirrors a real account; parent co-signs; §7).

**Diegetic (ranger):** budget/jar/opportunity-cost mechanics reskin as managing the reserve's funds
and grants; compounding/EV/percent stay clean meta puzzles.

## 5. Deep-dives (the math we model)

**Compounding.** FV = P(1+r)ⁿ; with contributions FV = P(1+r)ⁿ + C·[((1+r)ⁿ−1)/r]. Intuition to
*build*: interest joins principal, so the base grows, so next period's interest is larger. **Rule of
72** (years to double ≈ 72 ÷ rate%) is the kid-sized handle *once percentages are fluent*. Fees are
the same machine in reverse. Make it **felt** via predict-then-reveal + slow deterministic visual
growth (tree/snowball); **never** a random payout or escalating bonus multiplier.

**EV / risk.** EV = Σ(outcomeᵢ × probᵢ); variance = Σ pᵢ(outcomeᵢ − EV)². **Risk of ruin:** a
positive-EV bet still hits zero if you stake too much, and zero is absorbing. **Kelly intuition** (not
formula, for a child): *"a good bet can still ruin you if you bet too much of what you have."*

## 6. The bright line — honest EV vs gambling-shaped (mandatory, repo-wide)

Educational EV requires **explicit odds · transparent outcomes · reason-before-act ·
capped/non-escalating attempts**. **VETO** anything with **variable-ratio reinforcement** (rewards
after an unpredictable number of tries — the schedule most resistant to extinction, the engine of
slot machines and loot boxes), **near-miss** displays, **hidden odds**, **loss-disguised-as-win**, or
**double-or-nothing** loops. This applies to the *whole game*, not just finance (PLAN guardrail).

## 7. Graduation playbook (sim → real money) — feeds PLAN §7 decision 4

**Readiness signals (look for several):** completes multi-week saving goals unprompted; clears
Percent Forge (the gate); can explain why a broad index beats frequent trading and what a fee does;
chooses the lower-variance option when ruin is possible and says why; shows the behavior *outside* the
game.

**How to add real stakes safely:** (1) real savings account in the child's name, balance visible;
(2) allowance as the teaching instrument, separate from chores, with a recurring calm money
conversation; (3) co-managed broad index fund ~13–16, parent co-signs every decision, game logs the
reasoning; (4) pre-commit rules ("we don't sell on a scary week") agreed in advance; (5) the **18
deadline**.

**Dutch/EU specifics (*zeker* on mechanics):** minors can't invest independently (not
*handelingsbekwaam*); opening a brokerage account in the child's name needs a *machtiging* from the
*kantonrechter*. **On the 18th birthday the account legally becomes theirs and the parent loses all
control — a hard, real graduation deadline.** Child-account providers in broad low-cost index funds
include **Brand New Day** (kinderrekening, ~0.49% fee on the neutral 50/50 profile, free to open),
ABN AMRO, Rabobank, ASN, RegioBank, Meesman. Tax-free gift limits 2026: parents €6,908/yr,
grandparents €2,769/yr. A minor's assets count in the parent's Box 3. Investor protection €20,000
(*Beleggerscompensatie*). To delay full access past 18: *schenken met beheer* (gift-with-control).

## 8. Honesty lines — what we must NOT claim

- **Behavior-transfer is the weak link** — knowledge moves, behavior barely. No outcome promises.
- **"Money habits set for life by age 7" is overstated** — *foundations* form early, not fixed habits.
- **Allowance literature genuinely conflicts** (Mandell vs others) — chores/allowance separation is a
  prudent default, not a proven prescription. No research-backed amount ("€1/age/week" is folklore).
- **Marshmallow reappraisal is itself contested** (partial replication, not refutation) — patience
  matters, just less deterministically than folklore.
- **Kelly/EV math is adult-derived** — keep it to intuition for a child.
- **The "too-early compounding" trap is a reasoned synthesis**, strongly supported by the
  proportional-reasoning/illusion-of-linearity literature but not a single directly-tested finding.

## 9. Accessibility (dyslexia + dyscalculia-adjacent, number-heavy)

Audio-first; never gate on reading speed; TTS everywhere. Visual-first, low text load (icons,
manipulatives, 45–65 chars/line, line-height ≥1.5, left-align, no italics/ALL-CAPS, body ≥12–14pt
user-scalable; spacing matters more than the specific font). **Number-specific:** space digits in
long numbers (1 000 000); pair every numeral with a visual magnitude (bar/stack); choose fonts that
distinguish b/d, I/l/1, 6/9. **Dyscalculia-adjacent:** don't assume number sense; anchor every
quantity to a concrete visual; **no timed arithmetic**; unlimited penalty-free retries; show place
value; let the child **manipulate** quantities (drag/stack) rather than type. Reduce memory load
(reminders not recall, autosave, replay narration, no flashing).
