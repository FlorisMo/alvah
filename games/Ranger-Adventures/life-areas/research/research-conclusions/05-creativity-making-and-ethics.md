## 1. Verdict — what we build on

1. **Build creativity as generativity + taste over a portfolio, and ethics as reflection-not-scoring**
   (*zeker on the design stance*). The evidence supports divergent→convergent cycling, *trainable*
   critical/aesthetic judgement, and dilemma discussion — but is BLUNT that most creativity-training
   and character-education programs show **weak transfer beyond the trained task**. Design for durable
   *habits* (portfolio, critique ritual, reflection artefacts), never for "we taught creativity/ethics."
2. **Taste is the scarce AI-era skill, and it's trainable** (*waarschijnlijk*; design-education
   observation, not RCTs). When generation is cheap, *evaluation* is the human bottleneck — and
   judging a maker's work is the *same muscle* as judging AI output. This is the flagship build (§6).
3. **Constraints provoke creativity on an inverted-U** (*zeker within limits*; Acar 2019, 145 studies).
   A maker game built on *themed/limited* challenges beats blank-canvas freedom — but tune the
   constraint to each child's "Goldilocks" zone (too many stifle).
4. **Ethics must be pluralist and un-scored** (*the anti-indoctrination core*). Hold **care** (Gilligan),
   **consequences**, and **fairness** (Kohlberg) *together*; honour Haidt's intuition-first reality
   (notice the gut, then reason); never rank one lens as superior, never teach a single doctrine as
   "the answer." **Dilemma discussion is the best-evidenced method** (*zeker* method > passive info) —
   modest effects, weak transfer.
5. **The growth-mindset/praise line is half-true — implement it narrowly** (*zeker* on the caveat).
   Process-praise > person-praise is reasonably robust *for children* (*waarschijnlijk*), but mindset
   *interventions* have near-zero average academic effects (Macnamara 2022: non-significant among
   best-practice studies), concentrated only in disadvantaged learners. Use specific, task-level,
   process feedback; avoid "everyone's a genius" inflation — and remember **>⅓ of feedback
   interventions *decrease* performance** (Kluger & DeNisi) when they turn attention to the *self*.

## 2. What this trains, and why (design intent)

Two linked areas answering one family of question — *what is worth making, and what is worth doing*
(generativity + values), the human core that doesn't get cheaper as AI gets stronger. **Creativity &
making:** generate ideas worth having and build them, and develop *taste* (tell good from bad,
including good-from-bad AI output). **Ethics:** what is worth doing and what we owe others — *judgement
to develop*, not rules to obey. Diegetic fit: creativity is a **mix** (design a real solution for the
reserve in-world; open maker challenges as a meta studio); ethics is **diegetic-leaning** (the ranger
faces genuine dilemmas — cull vs. let-suffer, access vs. habitat, report a neighbour).

## 3. The spirals 7→21 (two strands)

**Creativity & making**

| Age | Skill | What it is | Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7–8 | Fluent generation | Produce MANY ideas/sketches fast without judging ("fill the page with 10 monsters") | DT high here; training gains rarely transfer — habit-building, not "boost." *Waarschijnlijk* |
| 8–9 | Generate→pick | Make many, then choose one to develop | Convergent thinking just emerging (Duval 2023); keep criteria few + concrete |
| 9–10 | Working with constraints | Themed/limited challenges ("only 3 blocks / 2 colours") | *Zeker* constraints help within the inverted-U; tune difficulty |

| 12–14 | Self-critique vocabulary | Name what works/doesn't using shared criteria | Adolescents may read process-praise as "adult thinks I'm low ability" — keep critique peer/self-driven |
| 13–15 | Domain specialisation | Go deeper in 1–2 media for a portfolio | Creativity becomes domain-specific (Barbot); breadth won't carry across media. *Zeker* |
| 15–17 | Critique of others' & AI work | Evaluate external work against intent | The scarce AI-era skill; *waarschijnlijk* (observation, not RCT) |
| 16–18 | Original intent / "voice" | Define what THIS work is trying to say before making | Creative identity consolidates in adolescence; support, don't impose |
| 18–21 | Portfolio curation & standards | Select/sequence best work; articulate a personal quality bar | No evidence a game yields professional creativity; frame as self-direction scaffolding |

**Ethics**

| Age | Skill | What it is | Flag |
|---|---|---|---|
| 7–8 | "Who is affected?" mapping | List everyone/every animal touched by a choice | Perspective-taking trainable; effects small–medium, measure-dependent |
| 8–9 | Noticing the gut feeling | Name the first reaction AND that it's a feeling, not yet a reason | *Zeker* judgment is often intuition-first (Haidt); not "feelings = answers" |
| 9–10 | Care vs. fairness tension | "Be kind to this one" and "be fair to all" can collide | Hold Gilligan + Kohlberg together; **do NOT rank one superior** |
| 10–12 | Consequences over time | Trace short- vs. long-term effects of an intervention | Real outcomes are uncertain; **model uncertainty, don't fake clean answers** |
| 11–13 | Dilemma discussion | Argue multiple positions; **no "correct" verdict required** | *Zeker* method > passive info; modest effects, weak transfer |
| 12–14 | Steel-manning the other side | State the strongest version of a view you disagree with | Guards against single-doctrine moralising; little child-RCT evidence |
| 13–15 | Stakeholder / justice analysis | Weigh access vs. protection; whose voice is missing? | Maps to Natura 2000 tensions; reasoning, not scoring |

## 4. Frameworks we'll use (evidence + veto status)

Veto column is Floris's (PLAN §7.1); blank = not yet ruled. Single-doctrine systems flagged ⚠.

**Creativity:** Divergent→Convergent, Guilford/Runco (strong *process model*; training transfer weak —
8–21) · Creative Problem Solving, Osborn–Parnes (best-supported training historically, small samples —
10–18) · Creativity-from-Constraints, Acar/Stokes (*moderate, well-theorised*, inverted-U — 9–21) ·

**Ethics:** **Philosophy for Children (P4C)** — community of enquiry, child-led questions (*moderate

prioritise it**) · Dilemma discussion, Blatt-Kohlberg/DIT (best-evidenced *method*; modest, transfer-

Haidt social intuitionism + Moral Foundations (descriptive, well-cited; innateness contested — 13–21) ·
**? Kohlberg stages** — ⚠ *if used as a ladder to "climb"* (Western, male-biased, weak behaviour link —
12–21; use as a *descriptive* shift, not a hierarchy) · **? Values clarification** — ⚠ weak evidence;
drifts into relativism/preachiness if done badly (12–18) · **? Virtue/Character education** — ⚠
single-doctrine risk, weak transfer (7–14) · **✗✗ Utilitarian / deontological single-frame teaching** —
⚠⚠ **indoctrination risk; flag for exclusion as a *sole* frame** (16–21; fine as *one lens among
several*, never "the answer").

## 5. Game mechanics to build

**Both areas are Model B. Maker seeds use a PORTFOLIO model; ranger seeds use a REFLECTION-ARTEFACT
model. No morality score anywhere.**

**Maker (portfolio):**
- **Ten-Monster Sprint** — fluent generation; timer, fill the page, no judging. 7–9 quantity only →
  12+ add a self-chosen "keeper" + why. *All sheets to portfolio; child tags keepers.*
- **The Keeper Pick** — divergent→convergent; generate 8, choose 1, justify against 2 concrete
  criteria (criteria count rises with age). *Portfolio stores the set + rationale.*
- **Three-Block Build** — constraint-leveraging; build something expressive under a hard material
  limit; tighten/loosen to find the child's inverted-U. *Photo + "what the limit forced me to do."*
- **Ugly First Draft** — iteration; deliberately rough v1 → v2 → v3, one change per pass. *Version
  history in portfolio shows the arc.*
- **Critique Cards** *(taste — flagship, see §6)* — rate a piece on a shared rubric (intent, craft,
  surprise), one strength + one "what if." 10+ adds exemplars and AI outputs. *Critique notes saved.*
- **Spot the AI Slop** *(AI-era taste)* — AI generates 3 options; child picks which serves the intent
  and says why the others fail; older = rewrite/repair the best. *Decision + reasoning to portfolio.*
  **Shares the evaluation muscle with [01-logic-math-epistemics.md](01-logic-math-epistemics.md)'s
  "Spot the Confident Robot."**
- **Voice Statement** — original intent; before making, write one line "this is trying to ___,"
  revisit across the project. *Intent statements paired with finished pieces.*

**Ranger dilemmas (reflection artefact — never scored):**
- **Who's Affected?** — moral imagination; map every affected party (deer, birds, visitors, neighbour,
  future animals). *A "stakeholder map" artefact, never scored.*
- **Gut-then-Reasons** — intuition-awareness (Haidt); log first reaction, then 2 reasons supporting +
  1 challenging it; older add a moral-foundation label. *Reflection-journal entry.*
- **The Intervene Dial** *(flagship dilemma, see §7)* — consequences + care/let-be; set an action on a
  slider (do nothing → feed → cull), narrate predicted outcomes + uncertainty. *Decision-log artefact;
  outcomes shown as plausibly-uncertain, never "right/wrong."*
- **Access vs. Habitat** — stakeholder/justice; open/close a trail, weigh visitors' joy & funding vs.
  disturbance; add competing legitimate voices. *A "decision brief" artefact.*
- **Steel-Man the Other Ranger** — perspective-taking; argue the position you DON'T hold as well as
  you can. *Written steel-man saved.* (Shares the steelman move with epistemics, area 2.)
- **Reporting a Neighbour** *(age-gated 12+)* — care vs. rules/loyalty; a neighbour breaks a rule (e.g.
  baiting wildlife); reason through loyalty, harm, fairness. *Reflection artefact only; the game never
  states the "correct" choice.*
- **Should This Be Made?** *(Maker+Ethics, 14+)* — the AI-era "should"; a project has a hidden cost,
  decide whether/how to proceed and attribute any AI help. *An attribution + reflection note.*
- **Seasons Replay** — long-horizon consequences; revisit a past decision a "year" later, see the
  uncertain outcome, re-reason. *Paired before/after reflection artefacts (growth visible, unscored).*

**Build dependencies / gates:**
- **Death/culling/harm content is hard age-gated** (§8) and tone-checked against the veluwe safety
  chart *before* it ships. Sequencing is a build constraint, not a content note.
- The **true-reaction** versions (a critique partner reacting to a finished piece; a dilemma character
  pushing back; "Spot the AI Slop") depend on the **§4b AI-simulation engine**. These are
  *lower-sensitivity* (about a *made thing* or a *reserve dilemma*, not Alvah's psyche) → cloud Claude
  acceptable on Floris's opt-in + safety rails (PLAN §4b/§7.6). The AI may inform a *portfolio* Model-B
  rung but **never scores ethics** — an AI grading a child's moral reasoning is exactly the line we
  don't cross (PLAN §4b).

## 6. Flagship deep-dive — taste / critique that doesn't crush a young maker

Train *evaluation* (the scarce AI-era skill) without crushing the maker. Evidence-informed principles:

- **Separate "generate" mode from "judge" mode.** Never critique during generation — protects fluency,
  honours the divergent/convergent separation.
- **Critique the work, not the person.** Process/task language only (Mueller & Dweck; Kluger & DeNisi's
  finding that ego-directed feedback *harms* — the same reason the agency brief bans person-praise).
- **Use a small, shared rubric** (intent / craft / surprise). *Consistency of judgement* is what
  aesthetic-education studies actually improve.
- **Always pair "one strength + one what-if."** Avoid pure praise (meaningless inflation) and pure
  deficit (discouragement).
- **Train on others' and AI's work first** — emotionally safer to judge external output, and it's
  *literally the same muscle* as judging AI. Then transfer to self-critique. This is the explicit seam
  with epistemics (area 2): the "evaluate the AI's answer" loop and the "evaluate this maker's work"
  loop reuse one critique engine.
- **Make taste visible over time** via the portfolio — the child *sees* their standards rise, which is
  intrinsically motivating and dodges "everyone's a genius."

## 7. Feedback design + the flagship dilemma (the Intervene Dial)

**Feedback rules (creativity side):** default to **process/strategy feedback** ("the way you reworked
the bridge made it sturdier"), not ability labels; be **specific and task-level** (ego-directed
feedback reduced performance in >⅓ of Kluger & DeNisi's cases); **tie feedback to a next action**
(gains concentrate at feedback + goal); **avoid inflation** (adolescents detect and discount empty or
process praise — shift toward peer/self-critique with age); **let failure be safe and iterative** so
feedback lands on a *draft*, not the child. **Do NOT apply any of this to ethics as scoring** —
ethical reflection gets acknowledgement and follow-up questions, never evaluative feedback or points.

**The Intervene Dial (flagship dilemma).** The slider (do nothing → feed → cull) is grounded in a real,
genuinely-contested case — the **Oostvaardersplassen** rewilding (a fenced ~56 km² reserve, no
predators or migration; in 2017–18 ~60% of the herd perished, ~90% of the dead shot by rangers as too
weak to survive; it split the Dutch public). Three *legitimate competing* framings — "let nature be"
(wildness) · "manage the population" (early culling) · "care for individuals" (welfare; the "fourth C:
Compassion") — map exactly onto intervene-vs-let-be. Because *experts themselves disagree*, the
mechanic **presents multiple legitimate framings, never a house view**, and shows outcomes as
**plausibly-uncertain, not right/wrong**. This is how the game tells the truth that real dilemmas have
no single answer — without lying that nature is simple.

## 8. Emotional safety / age-gating (heavy ethical content)

- **Hard-gate death/culling/harm content.** Eco-anxiety is real (Léger-Goodes 2022; Hickman) and
  **powerlessness drives the distress.**
- **Sequence agency BEFORE bleak facts** (Sobel): give the child tools to *act* and a sense of agency
  before exposing harsh realities, or hopelessness results.
- **Suggested gates:** **7–9** — no death/culling; care, habitats, "who's affected." **10–11** — natural
  death and population pressure, gently and abstractly. **12+** — culling/intervention dilemmas
  (Oostvaardersplassen-style), with the option to *step back*. **14+** — "reporting a neighbour,"
  harder moral conflict.
- **Always pair hard content with agency + a calm-down/normalising step** (name the emotion;
  group/parent discussion) — the hope-based-learning literature supports check-ins + journaling + a
  doable action.
- **Co-play flag:** route the heaviest dilemmas to **parent-present sessions** (the area-6 co-regulation
  lever applies here too — see [04-inner-work-communication-regulation.md](04-inner-work-communication-regulation.md) §7).
- **Every dilemma is tone-checked against the veluwe safety chart before it becomes content.**

## 9. Honesty lines — what we must NOT claim or do

- **Transfer is the recurring weakness.** Creativity-training and character-education effects are
  inflated by publication bias (creativity-training adjusted d≈0.29–0.32 after correction) and often
  don't transfer beyond trained tasks. A game can build *habits and enjoyment*; "raises general
  creativity or moral behaviour" is **unsupported** (*zeker* limitation).
- **Reasoning ≠ behaviour.** Moral-reasoning gains correlate *weakly* with real moral action (Kohlberg
  critique). The game targets *judgement*, not conduct — don't claim conduct.
- **Growth-mindset interventions are near-null on average** — meaningful effects concentrate only in
  low-achieving/disadvantaged learners. Use process-praise *narrowly*; it's shakier for adolescents/
  adults. (Shared honesty line with the agency brief's reward caution.)
- **No fourth-grade slump as fact** — the rigorous meta-analysis (Said-Metwaly 2021) found a
  *seventh*-grade dip (~12–13), not the popular fourth-grade one. Scaffold harder at ~12–13.
- **AI-ethics-for-kids is pre-evidence** — UNESCO/EU frameworks exist; validated child curricula
  essentially don't. Treat as best-guess design (*waarschijnlijk* at best).
- **Conservation specifics are contested** — even experts disagree on Oostvaardersplassen. Present
  multiple legitimate framings, never a house view.
- **No research on solo, tablet-based, longitudinal (7→21) designs** — dilemma-discussion evidence is
  group/classroom and short-term; solo-with-occasional-parent is an untested adaptation.

## 10. Accessibility (dyslexia)

Audio-first: **narrate all instructions and dilemmas; TTS on everything; accept spoken/drawn/built
responses, not just typed** — reduce reading load *without* lowering conceptual rigor (keep the
thinking hard, the reading light). **Spacing beats font choice** (≥1.5 line spacing, generous
inter-letter/word spacing, short lines — the better-evidenced lever); clean sans-serif with a toggle
(no consistent advantage for "dyslexia fonts" over good sans-serifs — let the child choose); ≥16px
body, user-scalable to 200%; avoid italics, ALL-CAPS, justified text, visual crowding; distinguish
mirror-pairs (b/d, p/q). Chunk text; icons + colour for navigation; **never gate progress on reading
speed.** (Consistent with the EF / area 1–4 a11y line: ≥56px targets, dual-channel feedback,
reduced-motion.)
