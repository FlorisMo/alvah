## 1. Verdict — what we build on

1. **Perceptual discrimination is genuinely trainable, but NARROW** (*zeker*). A skill trained on
   stimulus X improves X and little else (Karni & Sagi 1991: texture gains specific to retinal location
   *and* orientation; auditory learning at 100 ms doesn't transfer to 50/200 ms). Design for many
   *specific* wins (this bird, this track, this texture); the "general observation" claim is where
   pop-neuro overreaches — flag it for Floris in bold (§9).
2. **Expertise = perception + memory chunks, not better eyes** (*zeker*). Radiologists read an X-ray in
   ~200 ms; chess masters recall real boards but not random ones (~50,000 "chunks"). **This is buildable**
   — curated pattern libraries + spaced exposure — and is the honest model for "a ranger who reads the
   forest." Not superhuman senses; a big library of meaningful patterns and knowing where to look.
3. **Build TWO scoring engines, cleanly split** (*the core design call*). **Model A** (psychophysics
   staircase) for true discrimination/acuity — **reuse the EF adaptive staircase directly** (a 3-down/
   1-up rule targeting 79.4% correct is the cleanest fit, §6). **Model B** (portfolio) for awareness,
   slow-looking, sit-spots, journaling — **valuable but thinly evidenced; never reduce to a threshold or
   score**, or you kill the thing you're growing.
4. **The dyslexia-critical point** (*waarschijnlijk*). **Rhythm/musical-temporal training plausibly
   transfers to phonological awareness/reading** (Flaugnacco 2015 RCT: rhythmic reproduction best
   predicted phonological gains). **Birdsong/environmental-sound discrimination is a *different, separate*
   skill with NO evidence it touches phonology.** So: include rhythm/beat games (fun *and* possible
   reading spillover); include birdsong/soundscape for mastery + diegetic joy with **no reading claim**;
   **never frame any audio game as remediation** (risks pathologising and disappointment).
5. **Sensory systems mature on different clocks; early "deficits" are usually attention/memory, not
   sensory limits** (*zeker*). Acuity/colour adult-like by ~6–7, but pitch/voice JNDs keep falling into
   the teens. **Before ~10: reward engagement and breadth, many easy wins. Fine-threshold chasing belongs
   in the teen bands** — and poor early performance is *not* a sensory problem to diagnose.

## 2. What this trains, and why (design intent)

Training the senses — sight, hearing, smell, taste, touch — toward higher *resolution*, plus the
metacognitive layer: **becoming aware that you are perceiving at all**, slowing down, attending on
purpose. Includes **interoception as a perceptual skill**. The AI-era bet: perception is irreducibly
embodied — a machine cannot perceive reality *for* you; first-hand high-resolution sensing is the ground
beneath taste (area 7), careful observation (area 2 epistemics), and presence (area 6) — an antidote to
living entirely through screens and someone else's representation. **Strongly diegetic**: the whole
sensing curriculum lives natively in ranger work.

## 4. Frameworks we'll use (evidence + veto status)

Veto column is Floris's (PLAN §7.1); blank = not yet ruled. **Measured perceptual-learning methods are
kept clearly separate from contemplative sensing traditions** — both wanted, both labelled.

**Measured (psychophysics / domain learning):** 2AFC visual / pitch-timbre / tactile discrimination
(**zeker**; vision 11+, hearing 9–12+, touch 12+ ceilinged by finger size) · **olfactory training
protocol** (fixed odorant set, attentive, ≥12 wks — **zeker for function**, 9+) · **Sapere / TastEd**
(sense-isolation + flavour vocabulary — **strong, RCT-grade for acceptance**, 7+) · curated
pattern-chunk libraries (strong by analogy to expertise studies, 12+) · birdsong ID (strong as *domain*
learning, 10+) · rhythm/beat training (**moderate; possible phonology transfer — waarschijnlijk**, 7+).

**Contemplative (tradition, not proof — keep, but label and portfolio-score):** Project Zero slow-looking
routines (*See–Think–Wonder*, *Looking: Ten Times Two*) · Oliveros Deep Listening / soundwalk · John
Muir Laws nature journaling ("I notice / I wonder / it reminds me of") · Jon Young sit-spot (baseline &
alarms) · phenology logging · wildlife tracking & sign.

**Use-the-exercises-drop-the-theory:** **Edwards's 5 drawing skills** (edges, negative space, proportion,
shading, gestalt) work and teach *seeing over preconception* — but the **left-brain/right-brain framing
is debunked; it must never appear in game copy** (9+).

**Interoception measures (perception):** Jumping Jack Paradigm (child-appropriate, 5–9) · sensation-
vocabulary / body-mapping (low-risk, 7+) · MAIA-Y self-report (validated 7+, some weak subscales) ·
**? Schandry heartbeat-counting — validity contested (Murphy 2024); use as *play only*, never an accuracy
meter** (8+).

## 5. Game mechanics to build

**Cleanly split: A = psychophysics staircase (acuity/discrimination) · B = portfolio (observation/
awareness, never threshold-scored).**

- **"Name That Call"** — birdsong/timbre ID; hear a Veluwe call → choose from 2 (then 3, 4) candidates;
  scale by confusable species, shorter clips, background noise. **Model A.**
- **"What Changed Since Yesterday"** — visual change detection; two photos of the patch → tap the
  difference; subtler/more changes, shorter look. **Model A.**
- **"Read the Track"** — sign discrimination; match a print/scat to the animal (fox vs badger vs marten);
  similar species, degraded sign. **Model A** (ID) **+ B** (field log of real finds).
- **"Match the Texture"** — tactile/visual texture; match a sample to bark/moss/sand/heather; finer
  pairs. **Model A.**
- **"Slow-Look Journal"** — deliberate seeing + vocabulary; 30-s look → "I notice / I wonder / reminds me
  of" → draw+word; longer holds, richer vocab targets. **Model B.** (Flagship awareness mechanic, §7.)
- **"The Sit-Spot"** — baseline + alarm reading; return to a chosen spot, log who's about and the "mood"
  of the birds; detect alarm patterns, predict the unseen predator. **Model B.**
- **"Ranger's Nose"** — olfactory discrimination (real-world, parent-assisted); smell the week's scent
  set, name & rate; more/closer odours, blind tests. **Model A** (blind ID) **+ B** (scent diary).
- **"Beyond Like/Dislike" (Kitchen Patrol)** — taste vocabulary (Sapere); place a food on sweet/sour/
  bitter/salt/umami + texture wheel; blended flavours, blind reference tastes. **Model B → A** for blind
  discrimination ladders.
- **"Pitch Path"** — pitch-direction JND; two tones → "higher or lower?"; shrinking interval via
  staircase. **Model A.** (Flagship psychophysics mechanic, §6.)
- **"Rhythm of the Forest"** — beat-keeping/rhythm copy (woodpecker drums, cuckoo cadence); tap back the
  pattern; longer/syncopated. **Model A** (and quietly phonology-friendly).
- **"Body Weather Report"** — interoceptive noticing; after a walk/run, locate & rate body signals
  (breath, heartbeat, warmth); fainter signals, finer intensity scale. **Model B** (explicitly NOT a
  heartbeat-accuracy score). *Shares the "Body Weather Vane" lineage with area 4 — perception here,
  regulation there.*
- **"Soundscape Map"** — layered listening + localisation; stand still, place each sound on a compass map
  by direction & distance; more layers, closer sources. **Model B** (+ **A** for localisation pairs).
- **"Phenology Wheel"** — seasonal-change noticing; log firsts (first heather bloom, first frost, first
  returning migrant); subtler indicators, multi-year comparison. **Model B.**
- **"Spot the Hidden Animal"** — scene chunking / camouflage; find the roe deer/adder; better camouflage,
  shorter exposure. **Model A.**
- **"Spectrogram Detective"** (teen) — visual-auditory cross-coding; match call to its spectrogram;
  confusable species, noisy recordings. **Model A.**

**Build dependencies / reuse wins:**
- **Every Model-A game reuses the existing EF 3-down/1-up staircase** (§6) — minimal new machinery, the
  strongest architecture-thesis win in the whole expansion.
- **Birdcall/soundscape assets reuse the EF game's xeno-canto/Freesound pipeline** (check per-recording
  CC licence). Veluwe content is ready: the Dutch "Big Five" (fox, badger, roe deer, wild boar, red deer)
  + the resident wolf (since 2019), pine marten, adder; Avibase/Xeno-canto Hoge Veluwe checklists.
- **Real-world / parent-assisted loops** (Ranger's Nose, Kitchen Patrol, the sit-spot) are co-play by
  design — the area-4 co-regulation / parent-as-mentor lever applies (sit-spot is a *mentoring* tradition).

## 6. Flagship deep-dive — the psychophysics staircase (reusing the EF engine)

**Paradigms to build:** (1) **2AFC** ("which one?") — the workhorse, immune to response bias, kid-legible.
(2) **JND via an adaptive staircase** — the engine that finds the level the child can *just barely* do.
(3) optionally **same/different** for smell/taste where "which is stronger" is awkward.

**The transformed up-down staircase (Levitt 1971):** require a *run* of correct answers to step harder,
one error to step easier. Convergence: *pⁿ = 0.5*.
- **2-down/1-up → 70.7% correct** (√0.5).
- **3-down/1-up → 79.4% correct** (∛0.5). **← recommended:** comfortably above chance, feels like ≈4-of-5
  right to a child, trivially codable, and **it is what the EF staircase already does.**

**Implementation recipe (reuses the EF engine):** (1) pick a stimulus dimension with a difficulty knob
(tone interval in semitones; image difference in %; odorant dilution step; texture grit). (2) **3 correct
in a row → harder one step; 1 wrong → easier one step.** (3) larger step early, smaller after the first 2
reversals (García-Pérez guidance — the EF staircase already does this). (4) **threshold = average of the
last 6–8 reversal levels.** (5) stop after ~10–15 reversals or a trial cap (kids fatigue — cap it).

**When to upgrade:** **QUEST** (Watson & Pelli 1983) — Bayesian, places each trial at the posterior-mean
threshold estimate; **use it where trial-economy matters** (younger child, harder modality); free
JS implementations exist (jsQUEST/jsQuestPlus). **Skip PSI** (Kontsevich & Tyler 1999) — it also fits the
*slope*, which needs ~300 trials and which this game almost never needs. **Bottom line: reuse the EF
3-down/1-up (79.4%) everywhere; optionally swap in QUEST for trial-economy; skip PSI.**

## 7. Flagship deep-dive — the "noticing" / awareness mechanic (without making it a chore)

The danger: the moment you *score* "are you paying attention?", you destroy the exploratory stance that
is the whole point. So awareness is **pure Model B**. Principles:

1. **Portfolio, never threshold.** Reward *production and return* — did you make observations? did you
   come back to your sit-spot? — never correctness.
2. **One universal capture verb across all senses:** "I notice / I wonder / it reminds me of" — low-
   friction, audio-friendly, generative.
3. **Make richness visible, not ranked** — a growing field notebook, an expanding vocabulary cloud, a
   phenology wheel filling over seasons. *Collection mechanics, not leaderboards* (this is the §7
   motivation rulebook of [06-meta-teaching-and-tracking-7-to-21.md](06-meta-teaching-and-tracking-7-to-21.md)
   applied: informational/competence feedback, no controlling tokens).
4. **Deep-Listening's two attentions as a toggle, not a test:** "zoom in on one sound" vs "open out to
   the whole soundscape." The skill is *switching on purpose*.
5. **Cadence over intensity.** Short, frequent, *optional* prompts ("the heather's out — go smell it")
   beat long mandatory sessions. Streaks of *returning* are the metric (forgiving, per the rulebook).
6. **Parent-as-mentor mode.** Laws and Young are mentoring traditions; a "ranger and apprentice" co-op
   (parent + child at the sit-spot) is more faithful and durable than solo screen time.
7. **Wonder is the reward.** Surface a real mystery ("why did the birds all go quiet just now?") rather
   than a gold star. Curiosity is the trainable engine of attention.

*Diegetic gift for this specific boy: John Muir Laws is himself dyslexic, rescued academically by the
joy of observation — a powerful, age-appropriate role-model frame, difference reframed as a way of
seeing differently.*

## 8. Interoception — the perception/regulation boundary (shared with area 4)

Garfinkel 2015 validated three dissociable dimensions; **this brief owns *accuracy + attention*** —
noticing and **naming/describing** a bodily signal (breath, heartbeat, warmth, hunger, cold air, muscle
tension). **The moment the child is asked to label a feeling or *change a state*, it's the regulation
strand** ([04-inner-work-communication-regulation.md](04-inner-work-communication-regulation.md)). Clean
boundary: **"notice & describe" = here; "interpret the feeling & change your state" = there** (Body →
Emotion → Action belongs to area 4). Schaan 2019 shows the two are separable in children (interoceptive
accuracy had no link to emotion recognition, only marginal to regulation). Tracking here stays
**Model-B-light and private**, like inner work (PLAN §8). **Heartbeat-counting validity is contested
(Murphy 2024)** — any heartbeat mechanic is *playful body-attention, not a validated accuracy meter*.

## 9. Honesty lines — what we must NOT claim or do

- **Generalisation is the big one (bold for Floris).** *No good evidence* that training perception in one
  domain makes a person globally more observant. Every Model-A engine buys a *specific* skill. Sell
  breadth as **a habit and a value**, never a trained trait. The single most over-promised claim — and
  the transfer table in [06-meta-teaching-and-tracking-7-to-21.md](06-meta-teaching-and-tracking-7-to-21.md)
  §8 already flags Perception as "specific only."
- **Contemplative practices are under-evidenced** — Project Zero, Deep Listening, sit-spot, nature
  journaling are valuable *traditions* with thin causal proof. Keep them (Floris wants them), but
  **labelled** and **portfolio-scored**, never threshold-scored.
- **Edwards's neuro-theory is debunked** (left/right brain) even though her exercises work — strip the
  theory from any copy; keep the 5 skills.
- **No reading-remediation claim for audio games.** Rhythm *may* spill into phonology; birdsong does not.
  Never frame a sensory game as fixing reading — risks disappointment and pathologising.
- **Heartbeat-counting validity is contested** — playful body-attention only, never an accuracy score.
- **Early "deficits" are usually attention/memory, not sensory limits** — poor young-band performance is
  *not* a sensory problem to diagnose; design for it.
- **Child-specific evidence is thinner than adult evidence** for smell training, fine tactile
  discrimination, and interoception measurement — adapted from adult/clinical work; treat as *reasonable
  extrapolations*, not proven child interventions.

## 10. Accessibility (dyslexia — and this is a strengths-playing area)

**Audio-first by default** — lead with sound, voice, image; text is optional support (Cornell's
screen-reader-accessible *Bird Song Basics*, learnable entirely by ear, is the north-star). **Manage
visual vs textual load separately:** visual *perception* games are fine and even play to his strengths —
the load to minimise is *reading/decoding text*, not looking at images. Use icons, spoken instructions,
and the child's own **voice notes** ("speak out loud what you see") instead of written entries. **Handle
the auditory↔phonology interaction with care** (rhythm games may help; birdsong is for joy; *no
remediation framing*). **No deficit framing, ever — this is enrichment, not therapy** (Laws's
dyslexia-to-naturalist story is the model: difference as a way of *seeing differently*). Short sessions,
generous wins, child-paced (Sapere's "at their own pace" + the staircase's ≈4-of-5-right target both
protect motivation). (Consistent with the shared floor: ≥56px targets, dual-channel feedback,
reduced-motion.)
