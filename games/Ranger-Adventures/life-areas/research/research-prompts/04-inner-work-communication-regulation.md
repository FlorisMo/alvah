## PASTE THIS INTO DEEP RESEARCH

**Context.** I am building a long-horizon educational game for my son (currently 8, Dutch, dyslexic)
that grows with him for years. This brief is the **inner and relational** layer: knowing your own
parts and leading them, communicating truthfully without conflict, and regulating your body/nervous
system and attention. My primary lens is **Internal Family Systems (IFS)** — I already use "parts
with good intentions" as the house frame for our whole family project. For communication I lean
**Nonviolent Communication (NVC)**. I want self-regulation taught through **interoception and
nervous-system awareness**, not cognitive restructuring.

**HARD CONSTRAINT — please honour exactly:**
- **No CBT.** No thought-records, no "challenge/dispute your irrational thoughts," no cognitive
  restructuring framing. I have a considered dislike of CBT for this purpose. If evidence favours
  CBT for some outcome, you may *note* it neutrally, but do **not** build the curriculum on it.
- **No positive-thinking / affirmations** as the mechanism.
- This is **education and self-knowledge for a healthy child**, **not therapy** and not a clinical
  intervention. Frame everything as ordinary skill-building a parent can support, and flag clearly
  anywhere a topic crosses into territory that needs a professional rather than a game.

**Audience:** me (parent/designer), distilling into *light, mostly private* game mechanics and a
spiral curriculum across **ages 7 → 21**. Critical design value: I will **never reduce my child to a
score** — inner work especially must be tracked (if at all) as light, private reflection, never a
rating of his feelings or character.

**Research questions — go deep:**

1. **IFS for children — the developmental & practical picture.** How is IFS (parts, Self,
   protectors/exiles — Schwartz) adapted for kids? What is age-appropriate at 8 / 12 / 16? What is
   the honest evidence base for IFS generally, and specifically for non-clinical, parent-led,
   skill-building use? Where is the line a game must not cross (trauma, exiles) and hand to a human?
2. **Emotional literacy & interoception, ages ~7 to ~21.** Best-evidenced ways to build naming of
   feelings and *body sensations* (interoceptive awareness). Tools like emotion granularity, the
   "name it to tame it" idea, the felt sense (Gendlin's Focusing). What actually develops emotion
   regulation in children — and what is fluff?
3. **Nervous-system / co-regulation basics.** Age-appropriate, non-pathologising ways to teach
   states of arousal (calm / alert / overwhelmed) and down-regulation (breath, movement, rest),
   including the role of a parent's co-regulation. Polyvagal-informed *if* the evidence supports it —
   flag the evidence honestly, it is contested.
4. **Communication — NVC and beyond.** NVC's observation/feeling/need/request model (Rosenberg)
   adapted for kids; active listening / reflective listening; perspective-taking and theory-of-mind
   development; conflict repair. What is well-evidenced vs idealistic? How do I teach "say the true
   thing without a fight" at 8 vs 15?
5. **The shared tension loop.** I see Internal work + Communication + my self-organisation world as
   one loop: notice a tension (which *part* feels it) → articulate it cleanly (NVC, no blame) → make
   a request/proposal. Is there literature or credible practice unifying inner awareness with
   non-violent expression this way? How would I scaffold it for a child?
6. **Attention as trainable** (the Health overlap). Single-tasking, focus reps, mindfulness-for-kids
   — honest evidence on what builds sustained attention in children and what is overclaimed.
   Especially: protecting attention against engineered/digital distraction.
7. **Physical-health basics (smaller section).** Sleep, movement, food framed as *fuel for the
   things he wants to do* — non-shaming, no weight/calorie framing for a child. Just the
   highest-leverage, best-evidenced habits and how to make them felt rather than preached.

**Honesty constraints:**
- Tag **zeker** / **waarschijnlijk**. IFS, polyvagal, and much of SEL have **contested or thin**
  evidence — say so clearly rather than selling it. I want to use IFS *because I find it true and
  humane*, with eyes open about the evidence.
- Respect the CBT / not-therapy / not-positive-thinking constraints above throughout.
- Flag every point where a game should *stop* and a real human (parent, professional) should step in.

**Required output format:**
- **Rung-by-rung spirals**, ages 7→21, separately for *inner work*, *communication*, and
  *regulation/attention* (table: age band · skill · what it actually is · realistic flag).
- A **framework menu** per strand (name · one-line · evidence flag · best age · veto column blank),
  with CBT explicitly listed in an "excluded by request" row so the boundary is unambiguous.
- **10–15 game-mechanic seeds** that are *light and mostly private/diegetic* — e.g. the ranger
  meeting his own fear before a storm; an NVC choice when a farmer is angry — each with skill · loop ·
  difficulty scaling · and a **tracking note that is deliberately minimal** (presence/streak of
  reflection at most, never a rating of inner states).
- A **"never cross this line" section**: topics/mechanics a game must not attempt, and how to hand
  off gracefully to a parent or professional.
- A **co-regulation section**: where the *parent* is the mechanism, and how the game invites that.
- **Accessibility notes** (dyslexia; emotional content read-aloud) and **open-gaps / caveats**.
- **Sources** (cite; flag CC0/open material for clean-room rewrite).
